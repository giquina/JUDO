# Judo Safety Protocols

> For: University of London Judo Club - Coach Reference Guide

## 1. Concussion Protocol

### Immediate Response (CDC Guidelines)
**Source:** https://www.usjf.com/cdc-safesport/

#### Recognition Signs
- Headache or pressure in head
- Nausea or vomiting
- Balance problems or dizziness
- Double or blurry vision
- Sensitivity to light or noise
- Feeling sluggish, foggy, or groggy
- Concentration or memory problems
- Confusion
- Just not "feeling right"

#### Immediate Action
1. **STOP** - Remove athlete from training immediately
2. **ASSESS** - Check for danger signs
3. **INFORM** - Notify parents/guardians
4. **DOCUMENT** - Record incident details
5. **REFER** - Recommend medical evaluation

### Danger Signs (Call Emergency Services)
- One pupil larger than the other
- Extreme drowsiness or cannot be awakened
- Worsening headache
- Slurred speech, weakness, numbness
- Repeated vomiting
- Convulsions or seizures
- Increasing confusion or agitation
- Loss of consciousness

## 2. Return-to-Play Protocol

### 5-Step Graduated Return

| Step | Activity | Goal |
|------|----------|------|
| 1 | Light aerobic exercise | Increase heart rate |
| 2 | Sport-specific exercise | Add movement |
| 3 | Non-contact drills | Exercise, coordination |
| 4 | Full contact practice | Restore confidence |
| 5 | Return to competition | Normal participation |

**Rules:**
- Minimum 24 hours between each step
- If symptoms return, go back to previous step
- Medical clearance required before Step 4
- Minimum 7 days total recovery time

## 3. Age-Appropriate Technique Restrictions

### Under 13 Years
**Prohibited:**
- All choking techniques (Shime-waza)
- Neck cranks
- Certain throwing techniques with high impact

### Under 16 Years
**Prohibited:**
- Arm locks (Kansetsu-waza)
- Elbow locks (Juji-gatame variants)
- Shoulder locks (Ude-garami)

### All Ages
**Always Prohibited:**
- Techniques attacking the spine
- Leg locks (in traditional judo)
- Striking techniques

## 4. Injury Prevention

### Before Training
- [ ] Proper warm-up (minimum 10 minutes)
- [ ] Dynamic stretching
- [ ] Mat inspection (no gaps, tears, or wet spots)
- [ ] Remove all jewelry
- [ ] Trim fingernails and toenails
- [ ] Check judogi for tears

### During Training
- [ ] Proper breakfalls (ukemi) before throws
- [ ] Progressive intensity
- [ ] Adequate rest between rounds
- [ ] Hydration breaks
- [ ] Partner matching by size/skill
- [ ] Supervision at all times

### After Training
- [ ] Cool-down stretching
- [ ] Report any injuries
- [ ] Ice any minor impacts
- [ ] Rest and recover

---

## 5. Common Injuries & First Aid

### Sprains/Strains
- **RICE:** Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation
- Do not continue training
- Refer to medical professional if severe

### Dislocations
- Do NOT attempt to relocate
- Immobilize the joint
- Apply ice
- Seek immediate medical attention

### Cuts/Abrasions
- Clean wound thoroughly
- Apply pressure to stop bleeding
- Cover with sterile bandage
- Must be covered to continue training

### Head Impacts
- Follow concussion protocol
- When in doubt, sit them out
- Document all head injuries

### Emergency Action Plan
1. **Assess** the situation safely
2. **Call** for help if needed (999)
3. **Care** for the injured person
4. **Clear** the area of other students
5. **Contact** emergency contact/parents
6. **Complete** incident report

### Required Equipment
- [ ] First aid kit (stocked and accessible)
- [ ] Ice packs
- [ ] Defibrillator (AED) - if available
- [ ] Emergency contact list
- [ ] Incident report forms

## 7. Mat Safety

### Pre-Training Check
- Mats properly connected (no gaps)
- Mats clean and dry
- No debris or hazards
- Adequate space around edges
- Proper lighting

### Mat Standards
- IJF approved mats recommended
- Minimum 40mm thickness for training
- 50mm+ for competition
- Replace damaged or worn mats

## 9. Safeguarding

### British Judo Requirements
- All coaches must have DBS check
- Safeguarding training required
- Follow British Judo safeguarding policy

### Club Policy
- Two-adult rule for minors
- No unsupervised one-on-one training
- Appropriate physical contact only
- Clear communication with parents
