## Executive Summary

This document outlines a comprehensive charitable foundation initiative for the JUDO platform, designed to give back to the community by supporting homeless individuals through martial arts programs. Research demonstrates that martial arts training provides significant mental health benefits, community integration, and pathways out of homelessness. By integrating a charitable arm into the JUDO platform, we position ourselves as socially responsible leaders while creating measurable impact in our communities.

**Key Highlights:**
- Proven model: Programs like Fighting Homelessness (UK) show 76% improvement in stress/anxiety and 62% improvement in quality of life
- Legal framework: UK charity registration available for sports organizations with income over £5,000
- Funding model: 1-2% of membership revenue + donation matching + sponsorships
- Platform integration: Seamless donation tracking, volunteer coordination, and impact reporting built into existing JUDO platform

## 1. Foundation Structure

### 1.1 Recommended Model: Charitable Incorporated Organisation (CIO)

**Structure: Public Charitable Foundation with CIO Status**

Based on research into UK charity law and sports organization models, we recommend establishing a **Charitable Incorporated Organisation (CIO)** under UK Charity Commission registration.

**Why CIO?**
- Limited liability protection for trustees
- Simplified governance structure compared to companies
- Specifically designed for charities operating in England and Wales
- Lower administrative burden than charitable companies
- Recognized charitable purpose: "advancement of amateur sport for public benefit"

### 1.2 Governance Structure

**Board of Trustees (5-7 members):**
- JUDO Platform Founder/CEO (ex-officio)
- 2 Club Representatives (rotating appointments)
- 1 Judo Technical Expert (black belt, teaching experience)
- 1 Social Services Professional (homeless sector experience)
- 1 Financial/Legal Expert
- 1 Independent Community Representative

**Advisory Council:**
- Representatives from partner homeless charities
- Medical/mental health professionals
- Local government liaison
- Student representatives from University of London

**Operational Team:**
- Foundation Director (part-time, 0.6 FTE)
- Volunteer Coordinator (part-time, 0.4 FTE)
- Administrative support shared with JUDO platform

### 1.3 Legal Requirements & Registration

**UK Charity Commission Registration:**
- Required when annual income exceeds £5,000
- Registration timeline: 3-6 months
- Annual reporting requirements to Charity Commission
- Public accountability through published accounts

**Charitable Objects (Draft):**
1. To advance amateur sport, specifically judo and martial arts, for the public benefit
2. To relieve poverty and homelessness through the provision of free judo training, equipment, and community support
3. To advance health and wellbeing through physical activity and mental health support
4. To promote social inclusion for people disadvantaged by homelessness

**Legal Considerations:**
- DBS checks for all volunteers working with vulnerable adults
- Public Liability Insurance (£5-10 million coverage)
- Safeguarding policies compliant with Charity Commission guidance
- Data protection compliance (UK GDPR) for beneficiary information
- Risk assessments for all programs and venues

### 1.4 Financial Framework

**Bank Account:**
- Separate charitable bank account required
- Minimum 2 trustees as signatories
- Clear separation from commercial JUDO platform finances

**Accounting Standards:**
- Comply with Charities SORP (Statement of Recommended Practice)
- Annual independent examination or audit (dependent on income level)
- Transparent reporting of income sources and expenditure

**Tax Benefits:**
- Gift Aid eligible (adds 25% to UK taxpayer donations)
- Business rates relief on premises (up to 80% mandatory, 20% discretionary)
- Corporation tax exemption on charitable activities

## 2. Program Ideas

### 2.1 Core Program: Free Judo Classes for Homeless Individuals

**"Fighting Forward" Program** (inspired by successful UK model Fighting Homelessness)

**Structure:**
- 10-week structured judo program (2 sessions per week)
- Beginner-friendly, no prior experience required
- Sessions include warm-up, technique training, controlled sparring, cool-down
- Combined with brief mindfulness/goal-setting sessions (10 minutes per class)

**Locations:**
- Partner judo clubs (evening sessions when main programs complete)
- Community centers near homeless services
- University gym facilities (Birkbeck partnership opportunity)

**Benefits Delivered:**
- Physical fitness and coordination
- Mental health improvement (stress, anxiety, depression reduction)
- Structured routine and community belonging
- Goal-setting and achievement experience
- Self-defense skills and confidence building

**Success Story Model:**
Single Homeless Project's Sport + Health program achieved:
- 76% improvement in stress, anxiety, and depression levels
- 62% improvement in overall quality of life
- Increased social connections and community integration

### 2.2 Equipment Donation Program

**"Gi for Good" Initiative**

**Collection Program:**
- Donation boxes at all partner clubs
- Seasonal equipment donation drives (2-3 per year)
- Corporate sponsorships for new equipment purchases
- Equipment trade-in program for club members upgrading gear

**Distribution:**
- Free gi (uniform), belt, and training equipment for program participants
- Equipment loan program for ongoing training
- "Starter kits" for individuals transitioning to independent club membership

**Quality Standards:**
- Donated equipment cleaned, inspected, and repaired before distribution
- Partnered with laundry services for uniform cleaning
- New equipment purchased for participants completing programs

### 2.3 Partnership Programs with Homeless Organizations

**Strategic Partnerships:**

**Tier 1 Partners (London-based):**
- **Single Homeless Project (SHP)** - Existing Sport + Health program, proven model
- **New Horizon Youth Centre** - Focus on 18-24 year olds, education and training integration
- **The Passage** - Comprehensive homeless services, client referral pathway
- **Whitechapel Mission** - 149 years of service, established activity programs

**Partnership Value Exchange:**
- JUDO platform provides: Free training programs, equipment, volunteer coordination
- Partner provides: Client referrals, venue access, wraparound support services, safeguarding infrastructure

**Referral Pathway:**
1. Partner organization identifies suitable clients
2. Assessment meeting (program coordinator + client + case worker)
3. 10-week program enrollment with support plan
4. Ongoing check-ins with partner case workers
5. Post-program evaluation and transition planning

**Integrated Support Model:**
- Housing support coordination with partner services
- Mental health referrals through partner networks
- Employment and education pathway planning
- Continuation options: club membership scholarships, volunteer roles, coaching training

### 2.4 Scholarship Programs

**"Black Belt to Housing" Pathway Scholarships**

**Full Annual Membership Scholarships:**
- 50 full scholarships per year (target)
- Covers club membership fees for 12 months
- Available to program graduates transitioning to stable housing
- Renewable annually based on attendance and engagement

**Coaching Development Scholarships:**
- Subsidized Level 1 coaching qualifications for program alumni
- Creates employment pathways and community leadership roles
- Partners with British Judo Association for qualification recognition

**Competition Entry Scholarships:**
- Funding for local competition entry fees and travel
- Goal-setting opportunity and achievement milestone
- Mentorship from experienced club competitors

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- Completion of 10-week Fighting Forward program
- Demonstrable commitment to training
- Engaged with housing or employment support services
- Nomination from partner organization case worker

### 2.5 Community Outreach Events

**Quarterly Community Judo Days:**
- Free open sessions at partner clubs
- Demonstrations by experienced judoka
- Health and wellness information stands
- Collaboration with local homeless services for on-site support

**Annual "Judo for Change" Tournament:**
- Inclusive competition for all skill levels
- Teams of mixed experience levels (club members + program participants)
- Fundraising event with local business sponsorships
- Media coverage opportunity for awareness raising

**Homeless Awareness Campaigns:**
- "Sleep Out" fundraising events (club members sponsor)
- Social media campaigns highlighting success stories (with consent)
- Educational workshops at clubs about homelessness and social responsibility

**Volunteer Recognition Events:**
- Annual awards ceremony for outstanding volunteers
- Opportunities for volunteers to share experiences
- Networking with partner organizations

## 4. Funding Model

### 4.1 Revenue Streams

**Primary Revenue Stream: Membership Revenue Share**

**"1% for Change" Model:**
- 1-2% of all membership fees processed through JUDO platform directed to foundation
- Transparent disclosure to clubs and members at signup
- Optional increase by individual clubs or members

**Projected Income (Conservative Estimate):**
- Year 1: 500 memberships × £300 average annual × 1% = £1,500 foundation income
- Year 2: 2,000 memberships × £300 × 1.5% = £9,000
- Year 3: 5,000 memberships × £300 × 2% = £30,000
- Year 5: 15,000 memberships × £300 × 2% = £90,000

**Secondary Revenue Streams:**

**1. Direct Donations:**
- One-time donations at membership checkout (suggested £5, £10, £25)
- Monthly recurring donations (suggested £5/month)
- Gift Aid eligible (adds 25% for UK taxpayers)
- Projected: £10,000-£20,000 annually (Year 2+)

**2. Corporate Sponsorships:**
- Equipment manufacturers (gi, mat, training equipment)
- Sports nutrition brands
- Local businesses near participating clubs
- Tiered sponsorship packages: £1,000 (Bronze), £5,000 (Silver), £10,000+ (Gold)
- Projected: £15,000-£30,000 annually (Year 2+)

**3. Grant Funding:**
- Sport England: Community Sport Activation Fund
- National Lottery: People and Communities grants
- Local authority grants for homelessness initiatives
- Trust and foundation grants (Paul Hamlyn Foundation, Comic Relief)
- Projected: £20,000-£50,000 annually (Year 2+)

**4. Fundraising Events:**
- Annual Judo for Change tournament
- Sleep Out events
- Online crowdfunding campaigns
- Challenge events (e.g., 24-hour judo-a-thon)
- Projected: £5,000-£15,000 annually

**5. Donation Matching Programs:**
- JUDO platform matches member donations (up to £10,000 annually)
- Corporate matching programs (employers match employee donations)
- Amplifies donor impact and encourages participation
- Projected: £5,000-£15,000 annually

**Total Projected Annual Income:**
- Year 1: £10,000-£15,000 (foundation establishment + pilot)
- Year 2: £50,000-£80,000 (first full operational year)
- Year 3-5: £100,000-£200,000 (scaled operations)

**Diversified Income:**
- No single revenue stream exceeds 50% of total income
- Target 40% membership revenue, 30% grants, 20% sponsorships, 10% donations/events
- Build unrestricted reserves of 3-6 months operating costs

**Phased Growth:**
- Year 1: Seed funded by JUDO platform (£40,000-£50,000)
- Year 2: 50% self-funded, 50% external funding
- Year 3+: 80%+ self-funded through membership growth and established partnerships

**Grant Strategy:**
- Engage grant specialist consultant (part-time, Year 2)
- Apply for 10-15 grants annually
- Target success rate: 20-30% (2-4 successful grants)
- Focus on multi-year grants for stability

**Corporate Partnership Value:**
- Beyond financial contributions: in-kind donations (equipment, services)
- Employee volunteer programs
- Marketing and awareness amplification
- Long-term partnerships with shared values

**Platform Efficiency:**
- Shared administrative infrastructure with JUDO platform (lower overhead)
- Volunteer-led program delivery (lower staff costs)
- Technology-enabled coordination (reduced manual work)
- Partnership venue access (reduced facility costs)

## 5. Success Metrics & Impact Measurement

### 5.1 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

**Program Delivery Metrics:**

**Reach & Participation:**
- Number of participants enrolled per cohort
- Number of cohorts delivered per year
- Total unique individuals served annually
- Geographic coverage (number of partner locations)
- Demographic diversity (age, gender, background)

**Targets:**
- Year 1: 20-30 participants (2 cohorts)
- Year 2: 100-120 participants (6 cohorts)
- Year 3: 200+ participants (12+ cohorts)

**Engagement & Completion:**
- Program completion rate (% completing 10-week program)
- Average attendance rate across all sessions
- Participant satisfaction scores (post-program survey)
- Volunteer retention rate

**Targets:**
- 70%+ completion rate
- 80%+ attendance rate for active participants
- 4.0+ satisfaction (out of 5.0)
- 60%+ volunteer return rate for subsequent programs

**Progression & Continuation:**
- % of graduates continuing judo training (scholarship or self-funded)
- Number of participants achieving first belt promotion
- Number of graduates enrolling in coaching qualifications
- Number of graduates becoming volunteers/mentors

**Targets:**
- 30%+ continue training post-program
- 50%+ achieve first promotion within 12 months
- 5-10 graduates in coaching pathway by Year 3
- 10+ alumni volunteers by Year 3

### 5.2 Impact Metrics (Participant Outcomes)

**Mental Health & Wellbeing:**
- Pre/post program survey using validated tools:
  - ONS4 Wellbeing Questions (UK standard)
  - Warwick-Edinburgh Mental Wellbeing Scale (WEMWBS) - short form
- Self-reported changes in:
  - Stress and anxiety levels
  - Depression symptoms
  - Life satisfaction
  - Sense of purpose

**Targets (based on Single Homeless Project evidence):**
- 60%+ report improved mental wellbeing
- 50%+ report reduced stress and anxiety
- 70%+ report increased life satisfaction

**Physical Health:**
- Self-reported physical activity levels (pre/post)
- Achievement of fitness milestones
- Engagement with health services (through partner referrals)

**Social Connection:**
- Perceived social isolation scores (pre/post)
- Number of new social connections made through program
- Sense of belonging to judo community
- Participation in social activities outside training

**Housing & Stability:**
- Housing status at program start vs. 6 months post-completion
- Engagement with housing support services
- Stability of accommodation (number of moves)

**Employment & Education:**
- Engagement with employment support services
- Skills development (coaching qualifications, volunteering)
- Employment status at program start vs. 6-12 months post

**Targets:**
- 20% transition to more stable housing within 12 months
- 40% engage with employment/education services
- 15% secure employment or training placement within 12 months

### 5.3 Organizational Performance Metrics

**Financial Health:**
- Total annual income
- Income diversity (% from each revenue stream)
- Cost per participant served
- Fundraising return on investment (£ raised per £ spent)
- Reserve fund as % of annual expenditure

**Targets:**
- Year 2: £50,000+ income from 4+ sources
- Year 3: £100,000+ income with no source >40%
- Cost per participant: £600-£800 (Year 2)
- Fundraising ROI: 4:1 or better
- Reserves: 3-6 months operating costs by Year 3

**Partnership Effectiveness:**
- Number of active partner organizations
- Referral conversion rate (referred to enrolled)
- Partner satisfaction scores
- Collaborative initiatives launched

**Platform Integration:**
- Member donation participation rate
- Volunteer signup rate
- Clubs participating in programs
- User engagement with foundation features

**Targets:**
- 15% of members make donations
- 5% of platform users volunteer
- 25% of clubs host programs or donate equipment

### 5.4 Data Collection & Reporting

**Participant Data Collection:**
- Informed consent process at program enrollment
- Anonymized data for reporting and evaluation
- Pre-program baseline survey (demographic, wellbeing, housing, goals)
- Mid-program check-in (attendance, engagement, barriers)
- Post-program survey (outcomes, satisfaction, continuation plans)
- 6-month follow-up survey (sustained outcomes, life changes)

**Data Protection:**
- Separate secure database for participant information
- Access limited to authorized foundation staff only
- Compliance with UK GDPR and Charity Commission requirements
- Data sharing agreement with partner organizations
- Participant right to withdraw data at any time

**Reporting Schedule:**

**Internal (Board of Trustees):**
- Quarterly: Financial reports, program updates, risk register
- Annual: Impact report, strategic review, budget approval

**External (Charity Commission):**
- Annual Return submission
- Annual accounts and trustee report
- Serious incident reporting (as needed)

**Stakeholders (Public):**
- Annual Impact Report (published on website)
- Quarterly newsletter to donors and volunteers
- Case studies and success stories (with participant consent)
- Social media updates on program milestones

**Evaluation Approach:**
- External evaluation of pilot program (Year 1)
- Annual self-evaluation using Theory of Change framework
- Bi-annual independent review (Year 3+)
- Continuous improvement based on feedback and data

## 7. Risk Management

### 7.1 Key Risks & Mitigation Strategies

**Safeguarding Risks:**
- **Risk:** Vulnerable adults may be at risk during programs or through interactions
- **Mitigation:**
  - Comprehensive safeguarding policy aligned with Charity Commission guidance
  - All staff and volunteers DBS checked
  - Mandatory safeguarding training for all volunteers
  - Clear reporting procedures for concerns
  - Partnership with experienced homeless organizations for support
  - Risk assessments for all activities and venues

**Financial Risks:**
- **Risk:** Insufficient funding to sustain programs; over-reliance on single income source
- **Mitigation:**
  - Diversified revenue streams (membership, donations, grants, sponsors)
  - Conservative financial planning and cash flow management
  - Reserve fund building from Year 2
  - Grant applications to multiple sources
  - Annual budget review and adjustment

**Reputational Risks:**
- **Risk:** Negative incident or poor outcomes damage JUDO platform reputation
- **Mitigation:**
  - Strong governance structure with experienced trustees
  - Compliance with all legal and regulatory requirements
  - Transparent reporting and accountability
  - Serious incident reporting protocol
  - Crisis communication plan
  - Insurance coverage for liability

**Operational Risks:**
- **Risk:** Insufficient volunteer recruitment; volunteer burnout; venue unavailability
- **Mitigation:**
  - Structured volunteer recruitment and retention program
  - Reasonable time commitments and rotation schedules
  - Backup volunteer pool for continuity
  - Multiple venue options and partnership agreements
  - Foundation staff to coordinate and support volunteers

**Participant Engagement Risks:**
- **Risk:** Low enrollment; high dropout rates; participant safety concerns
- **Mitigation:**
  - Strong partnerships with referral organizations
  - Participant needs assessment and tailored support
  - Flexible program design to accommodate challenges
  - Wraparound support through partner case workers
  - Regular check-ins and barrier identification
  - Transport and meal support where needed

**Legal & Compliance Risks:**
- **Risk:** Non-compliance with charity law; data protection breaches; insurance gaps
- **Mitigation:**
  - Legal advisor support for charity registration and compliance
  - Annual independent examination/audit
  - UK GDPR compliant data systems and processes
  - Comprehensive insurance coverage reviewed annually
  - Trustee training on legal responsibilities

### Appendix B: Comparable Organization Case Studies

- Established program with proven outcomes:
  - 76% improvement in stress, anxiety, and depression
  - 62% improvement in overall quality of life
- Wide range of activities: football, boxing, rock climbing, aerobics, yoga
- Funded through Sport England's Active Ageing Initiative
- Partnership model with wraparound homeless services

**3. Level Up Brazilian Jiu Jitsu (Los Angeles)**
- Partnership with PATH (People Assisting the Homeless)
- Free classes for children from homeless families
- Integrated with International Jiu Jitsu Education Fund
- Demonstrates successful equipment provision and access model

**4. Mixed Martial Arts + Psychotherapy Integration (Research)**
- 10-week program combining MMA training with one-to-one counseling
- Target: Young males' mental health
- Outcomes: Improved relationships, work life, and self-esteem
- Key insight: Combination of physical activity AND therapeutic support maximizes impact
